Personal Career:

Kiran Peshawaria was born on 9 June 1949 in Amritsar, East Punjab, Dominion of India, into a Punjabi business family. She is the second child of Prakash Lal Peshawaria and Prem Lata (born Janak Arora).She has three sisters: Shashi, Reeta, and Anu. Bedi started her formal studies in 1954, at the Sacred Heart Convent School in Amritsar. She participated in National Cadet Corps (NCC), among other extra-curricular activities.Bedi graduated in 1968, with a Bachelor of Arts (Honours) in English, from Government College for Women at Amritsar. The same year, she won the NCC Cadet Officer Award. In 1970, she obtained a Master's degree in Political Science from Punjab University in Chandigarh. Later, during her career in the Indian Police Service, she also earned a Bachelors of Law degree from the Faculty of Law, University of Delhi in 1988 and a Doctorate of Philosophy from the Indian Institute of Technology - Delhi's Department of Humanities and Social Sciences in New Delhi in 1993. Inspired by her father, Bedi started playing tennis at the age of nine. As a teenage tennis player, she cut her hair short as it interfered with her game.[11] In 1964, she played her first tournament outside Amritsar, participating in the national junior lawn tennis championship at Delhi Gymkhana. She lost in early rounds, but came back to win the trophy two years later, in 1966.

Kiran Bedi got married to Brij Bedi,a tennis player on 9 march 1972.Their marriage took place shortly after she joined the IPS. Brij Bedi was also a decorated police officer. The couple has a daughter,Sukriti(Saina). Saina has led a more private life compared to her parents.Kiran Bedi's dedication to her career and public service often made her a prominent figure in the public eye. While her family life has not been as extensively covered in the media, she has balanced her responsibilities as a police officer, activist, and later a politician with her personal life. Kiran Bedi made history in 1972 when she became the first woman to join the Indian Police Service (IPS). Throughout her career, she held various positions in different regions of India, showcasing her dedication, courage, and innovative approach to law enforcement.After retiring from the IPS in 2007, she continued her work as a social activist. She founded the Navjyoti India Foundation, an NGO dedicated to addressing various social issues, including education, drug abuse prevention, and women's empowerment.

Major designations held:
  • Kiran Bedi made history in 1972 by becoming the First Woman IPS Officer.
  • Kiran Bedi served as the Deputy Commissioner of Police in charge of traffic in Delhi.
  • Kiran Bedi served as the Director General of the Narcotics Control Bureau.
  • Kiran Bedi served as the Deputy Commandant of Railway Protection Force in New Delhi.
  • Kiran Bedi was appointed as the Inspector General of Prisons at Tihar Jail in Delhi.
  • Kiran Bedi was appointed as the United Nations Civilian Police Adviser in 2003.
